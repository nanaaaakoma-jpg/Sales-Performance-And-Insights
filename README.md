# Overview
This project provides a high-level visualization of sales performance, focusing on total revenue, total quantity sold, category contribution, and monthly trends. 
It enables quick identification of top-performing product categories and items, while also showing seasonal sales fluctuations to support data-driven decision-making.

# Data Source
Kaggle

# Tools
MS Excel, Power Query, Power Bi

# Objectives
To monitor overall sales (both in revenue and quantity).

To identify best-performing and underperforming product categories.

To track monthly and seasonal sales variations.

To understand product-level contribution to total sales.

To support business decisions for inventory planning and marketing strategies.

# Problem Statement
Most businesses struggle to:

Consolidate visibility into sales performance across categories and time.

Identify top-performing products and revenue drivers.

Detect seasonal trends affecting sales.

Identify how quantity sold relates to revenue generation.

This project addresses these gaps by integrating sales data into a single interactive visual platform for better business intelligence.

# Data cleaning and manipulation
To ensure accurate analysis, the following data cleaning steps were applied in Excel and Power Query:
Removal of duplicate transaction records.
Handling of missing or null values in revenue, quantity, and category fields.
Standardization of product and category names (e.g., consistent naming for vegetables and items).
Conversion of numerical fields (revenue, quantity) into appropriate formats.
Aggregation of transactional data into monthly, category, and item-level summaries.
Validation of outliers in sales values to avoid distortion in insights.

Year-over-year analysis shows that while sales quantity increased from 137.43K in 2021 to 161.30K in 2022 (17.4% growth), revenue declined from 1.10M to 1.04M (5.5% decrease). This suggests that growth was driven by higher sales volumes rather than higher-value sales, resulting in a decline in average revenue generated per unit sold. Despite this, category rankings remained largely stable, with Flower/Leaf Vegetables maintaining market leadership across both years. The findings suggest the need to optimize pricing strategies, focus on high-performing categories, capitalize on peak-demand periods, and evaluate lower-performing categories such as Solanum to improve overall revenue growth and profitability.

# Recommendations
To enhance overall sales performance, the business should prioritize its high-performing product categories, particularly Flower/Leaf Vegetables, Capsicum, and Edible Mushroom, while ensuring sufficient inventory and targeted promotional support. Seasonal demand patterns should be leveraged to improve forecasting, inventory planning, and marketing effectiveness during peak sales periods. Additionally, regular monitoring of category and product performance will enable more informed strategic decision-making and resource allocation.

Given the increase in sales volume alongside a decline in revenue, management should review pricing strategies and product mix to improve revenue generation and profitability. Efforts should also be directed toward optimizing underperforming categories through targeted interventions and focusing on high-demand products that consistently drive sales. By balancing volume growth with value creation, the business can achieve sustainable revenue growth and strengthen its overall market performance.
